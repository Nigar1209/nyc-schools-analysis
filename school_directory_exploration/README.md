# School Directory Exploration

## Overview
This project explores the **NYC High School Directory dataset** to identify patterns in school distribution, size, accessibility, and program offerings across the different boroughs of New York City.

The analysis was conducted using a Jupyter Notebook and focuses particularly on the **unique characteristics of schools in Staten Island** compared to the other boroughs.

---

## Project Files

- **Dataset:** `high-school-directory.csv`
- **Jupyter Notebook:** `school_directory_analysis.ipynb`

---

## Key Insights

### Special Case: Staten Island Schools

The dataset reveals several distinct characteristics of schools in **Staten Island** compared with other NYC boroughs. This is notable given that Staten Island has a significantly smaller population.

**Key observations:**

- **Number of Schools**
  - Staten Island: **10 schools**
  - Other boroughs: **80–121 schools**

- **Average School Size**
  - Staten Island: **1847 students per school**
  - Queens: **1046 students per school**
  - Bronx: **490 students per school**

- **School Type Diversity**
  - Staten Island: **2 school types**
  - Other boroughs: **9–11 school types**

- **Program Availability**
  - Staten Island schools offer **more programs on average (5 per school)**.
  - Other boroughs average **1–2 programs per school**.

  This may suggest that **larger schools tend to offer more programs**, while **specialized schools focus on fewer programs**.

- **Accessibility**
  - **100%** of Staten Island schools are **functionally accessible**.
  - Other boroughs range from **64% to 79% accessibility**.

---

### Distribution of School Types

- The majority of **specialized schools (36)** are **CTE schools (Career and Technical Education)**.
- There are:
  - **8 all-girls schools**
  - **3 all-boys schools**
